Part One

The Opportunity

What Nether Labs Is

Nether Labs is building org-in-a-box - deployable organizations for lean teams. The product (working name: Steward) is designed to give a 1-3 person team the operational leverage of a 10-person org, without the headcount.

Not a chatbot. Not a prompt wrapper. A functioning executive layer - with real hierarchy, defined values, institutional memory, and operating principles pre-installed - designed to operate while you sleep.

You deploy Steward. It learns your structure. It supports your ops. It is designed to improve over time.

The Problem

Every solo founder and lean team faces the same ceiling: you run out of bandwidth before you run out of opportunity. Coordination breaks down. Context gets lost. You hire, onboard, manage, and lose time to overhead instead of building.

Steward is the third option. An org designed to know your values, your SOPs, your reporting structure, and your standards - targeting deployment in hours rather than months of onboarding.

Why now: The model capability is here. The tooling is mature. The missing piece is the organizational layer - the culture, hierarchy, and memory that makes a team function. That's what Nether Labs builds.

Why this approach: Any competitor can copy a tech stack. They cannot copy culture baked at the agent level. The moat is in the values, operating principles, and institutional memory - not the infrastructure.

What We're NOT Building (18-Month Window)

Scope discipline is an operating philosophy. These are deliberate exclusions.

  • Enterprise sales motion (long cycles, procurement overhead - Series B story)
  • Generic free-form agent builder (destroys the product metaphor)
  • Per-agent pricing (signals "tool"; we sell orgs)
  • Voice interfaces (not required for PMF)
  • Full financial/bookkeeping compliance (liability surface we don't carry)
  • Multi-language/localization (English-first until $3M ARR)
  • Consumer B2C product (dilutes ICP and brand)

"A team that knows what it's not building is a team that knows what it's building."

Competitive Moat

Today

Nether Labs ran this organization for itself for months before selling it. Competitors can copy the architecture. They cannot fake having run it.

Months 4-9

Every org on Steward generates data on what agents get used, what tasks get routed, what founders actually care about. This data loop is unavailable to competitors starting from zero.

Months 10-18

Marketplace network effects, integration lock-in, and institutional memory (agents that know a founder's business for 12+ months) create real switching costs.
